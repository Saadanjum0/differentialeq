Explanation:

A differential equation is linear if it can be written in the form:

\(a_n(x) \frac{d^ny}{dx^n} + a_{n-1}(x) \frac{d^{n-1}y}{dx^{n-1}} + \ldots + a_1(x) \frac{dy}{dx} + a_0(x)y = g(x)\)

where \(a_n(x), a_{n-1}(x), \ldots, a_0(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are functions of \(x\) only (not involving \(y\)).
